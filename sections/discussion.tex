\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Why T3 failures persist after the substitution hint.}
Every T3 target reduces to a single-function inequality that one
of the four drafters could close autonomously once the right
rewrite is in hand. Two operations are jointly required for
closure: producing the log-domain $\Phi$-substitution (or a
problem-specific sqrt manipulation), and selecting the Mathlib
lemma chain that discharges the re-encoded goal. The \textsc{Sub}
column of Table~\ref{tab:ablation} ($0$-$2/6$ per drafter)
isolates the second operation. The \textsc{Sub+Chain} column
($3$-$5/6$) supplies both. Harmonic Aristotle closes $6/6$
(\S\ref{sec:capability-ladder}) without either hint, using the
$\Phi$-substitution on some targets and a direct
\texttt{sqrt\_le\_sqrt}-family route on others. Aristotle is
under a different compute regime
(\S\ref{sec:methods-tiers}). The $6/6$ vs $0$-$2/6$ gap should
be read as a system difference, not as an isolated generalist
tactic-vocabulary bound. The \textsc{Sub+Chain} result suggests
supplying the lemma list narrows the generalist closure gap on
T3, conditional on the Aristotle-derived chain
(\S\ref{sec:methods-tiers}, hint-ablation paragraph).

\paragraph{Specialised provers and the Mathlib-outside gap.}
Goedel-Prover-V2~\cite{goedelprover2025}, trained on Mathlib,
surfaces this gap in its own reasoning trace: on
\texttt{etabetting\_nonneg} ($0 \leq \eta_{\mathrm{betting}}(b)$),
the model responds \emph{``the Lean 4 code provided does not define
etaBetting \ldots\ we cannot proceed with a meaningful proof.''}
Under single-shot, DSPv2~\cite{dspv22025} ($9/48$) outperforms
Goedel-V2 ($5/48$) because its autocomplete format fits a $10$ GB
GPU. Under agentic mode, Goedel-V2's self-correction training
activates and it reaches $13/48$, surpassing DSPv2 (detailed
hardware-constraint analysis in
Appendix~\ref{app:inference-format}).

\paragraph{Hint-response asymmetry.}
The T3 hint ablation (Appendix~\ref{app:hint-ablation-table}) shows
generalist closure rising from $0$--$1/6$ at \textsc{Base} to
$3$--$5/6$ at \textsc{Sub+Chain}, while DSPv2 closes only one target
across both hint columns. For mixed-drafter ensembles this suggests
routing lemma-rich prompts to generalists and specialised provers to
targets where their tactic prior matches the goal shape.
